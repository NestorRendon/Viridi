About

I am Nestor David Rendon, PhD — AI researcher and Artist. I believe that everything is connected and that everything is in motion. Each element belongs to others to some degree—along lines explored in fuzzy logic, where membership is partial rather than absolute. Words and sounds are abstract building blocks of languages, carried by waves and electromagnetic fields; the whole picture is dynamic, and every thing belongs to others in larger or smaller measure.

From an early age I loved exploring and contemplating these rhythms of nature. As I grew older, I came to see them as hidden patterns waiting to be uncovered. The curiosity to understand how things work, and the pleasure of sensing them, are what drive my path.

On one side, I strongly believe in science and apply it to grasp how the world is made. My path in engineering and artificial intelligence follows that principle: understanding complex systems and turning that understanding into tangible solutions. On the other, art has let me meet nature not only through reason but through feeling and contemplation. In Johann Wolfgang von Goethe’s words: “Art is the mediator of the ineffable; science, its interpreter.”

Here I bring together the science and art projects I have been part of—to share some of my questions and trajectories, in case friends or collaborators want to walk together on paths of discovery and of looking closely at the world. You can browse parallel strands of that work in the tabs — Datascience, Ecoacoustics and bioacoustics, Music, Blog & Publications (including a readable CV summary you can open from the list), Contact, and Others.

Datascience & AI

I am Nestor David Rendon, PhD — AI researcher and Artist with training in data science and statistical modeling, living in Lugano, Switzerland. I work on modular ML pipelines, conversational agents, LLM fine-tuning, and reliable deployment in production, with a strong thread in unsupervised learning for audio and soundscapes, deep learning for vision, and modeling of high-dimensional data. I care about software that stays understandable as models and teams grow: clear boundaries between components, reproducible experiments, and systems that people can maintain.

I am an AI researcher with a PhD in data science and statistical modeling (University of Antioquia, 2024, cum laude). Day to day I focus on modular ML pipelines, conversational agents, LLM fine-tuning, and running models reliably in production (including on AWS and GCP), with attention to monitoring, containerization, and disciplined version control.

Technically I work across Python (advanced), C++, R, SQL, and Bash; frameworks such as PyTorch, TensorFlow, FastAPI, scikit-learn, OpenCV, and pytest; and themes like deep learning for vision, unsupervised learning, statistical modeling, clustering, benchmarking, and documentation. Current research interests include software architectures for multi-agent systems and integrating LLMs into modular, maintainable systems.

Since 2020 I have been a vision engineer / data scientist at xFarm Technologies (Switzerland), building conversational agents and deep learning components for large-scale agricultural monitoring, from ingestion and preprocessing through training, validation, and deployment.

Ecoacoustics & bioacoustics

During my PhD at the University of Antioquia I developed unsupervised methodologies for soundscape analysis, including internal clustering validation based on Fréchet distance, and modular experimental frameworks for geospatial, audio, and time-series data at scale. Related work continued at the Laboratoire MIA (France, 2022–2023), where I built benchmarking tools and ML pipelines oriented to ecoacoustic datasets.

Applied threads include rain detection from acoustic recordings (Colombia de Colores / IES, 2017–2020) and broader statistical modeling of high-dimensional environmental data. Selected papers are linked under Blog & Publications.

Representative publications

Letting Ecosystems Speak for Themselves — mapping landscape acoustic heterogeneity (Environmental Modelling & Software, 2025); uncertainty-aware clustering validation (Engineering Applications of Artificial Intelligence, 2023); automatic acoustic heterogeneity identification (Ecological Indicators, 2022).
